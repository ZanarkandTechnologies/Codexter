# Best Of Worlds Gotchas

- Do not mistake popularity for fit.
- Do not adopt source file layouts without checking local repo conventions.
- Do not let one impressive source dominate when several sources converge on a
  simpler pattern.
- Do not compare vague categories; compare one workflow, capability, or artifact
  type.
- Do not use subjective quality as the only metric when binary assertions or
  mechanical checks are possible.
- Do not ignore maintenance cost. A feature that needs permanent upkeep should
  clear a higher value bar.
- Do not put raw research dumps into final plans. Keep sources traceable, but
  lead with decisions.
