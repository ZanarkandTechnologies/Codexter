# Brand/Product Register

Use this to decide what kind of visual ambition fits the surface.

## Product Register

Design serves repeated work.

- Prioritize scanability, speed, state clarity, and reliable controls.
- Use restrained color unless a data role earns a stronger palette.
- Keep interaction predictable.
- Use cards only when they clarify grouping or affordance.
- Loading, empty, error, success, disabled, and max-content states matter.

Examples: dashboards, settings, admin tools, editors, inboxes, workflows, forms.

## Brand Register

Design is part of the product promise.

- Prioritize memory, emotion, composition, and strong first impression.
- Use image, type, and motion as primary material.
- A committed or drenched color strategy can be appropriate.
- One memorable visual idea beats a pile of effects.

Examples: landing pages, launch pages, portfolios, venue/product pages, editorial pages.

## Register Test

Ask: will the same user repeat this surface many times to complete work, or is the surface mainly meant to persuade, orient, or be remembered?
