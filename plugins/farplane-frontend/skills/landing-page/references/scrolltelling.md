# Scrolltelling

Use this for cinematic or narrative one-page pages.

## Story Beats

1. Opening signal: product/place/person/object is unmistakable in the first viewport.
2. Tension: what changes, hurts, or becomes possible.
3. Mechanism: how the offer works.
4. Proof: real examples, numbers, product state, testimonials, or artifacts.
5. Conversion: direct next action.

## Section Rules

- Keep the H1 literal: brand/product/place/person name or offer/category.
- Put value props in supporting copy, not as vague headline poetry.
- Show a hint of the next section below the fold on common desktop and mobile viewports.
- Use cards only for repeated items or framed tools, not as page-section wrappers.
- Make media reveal the actual thing when inspection matters.

## Scroll Narrative

For pinned/scroll-scrubbed pages:

- Define named phases with start/peak/end progress.
- Prevent blank gaps between phases.
- Keep interactive controls reachable outside pinned-only gimmicks.
- Provide a reduced-motion version.
- Capture screenshots at narrative checkpoints.
